The Cue Stick

Setup

With the player in position to take a shot, they need a stick to hit with. A stick prefab was created using a 3D asset sourced from CGTrader. Spawning it is straightforward with a standard Unity Instantiate call.

The cue stick model (Source)

Two-Handed Grip

The stick is a two-handed object. The striking hand is the dominant hand and grabs the base of the stick at a predefined grip point. The anchor hand acts as a directional reference. The stick always points from the striking hand toward the anchor hand's position, and the anchor hand is free to move up and down to adjust the stick's angle.

To prevent the stick from spinning freely around its own axis, the striking hand's up direction is projected onto the plane perpendicular to the stick direction and used as a stable roll reference. This gives the stick a clean, predictable orientation that responds naturally to how the player holds the controller. 

void UpdateStickPosition()
{   
    if (activeStick == null || currentState == StickState.Inactive) 
        return; 
    
    Vector3 effectiveAnchorPosition = isAnchorModeActive ? frozenAnchorPosition : anchorHand.position;    
    Vector3 stickDirection = (effectiveAnchorPosition - strikingHand.position).normalized;    
    
    // Rotate the stick based on the striking hand's orientation. Used to control the roll of attached object    
    Quaternion handRotation = strikingHand.rotation;  
    Vector3 handUp = handRotation * Vector3.up; 
    Vector3 stickUp = Vector3.ProjectOnPlane(handUp, stickDirection).normalized;    
    
    activeStick.transform.rotation = (stickUp != Vector3.zero)    
        ? Quaternion.LookRotation(stickDirection, stickUp)    
        : Quaternion.LookRotation(stickDirection);    
    
    // grabPoint is a predefined Transform near the base of the stick prefab    
    Vector3 grabPointOffset = activeStick.transform.rotation * grabPoint.localPosition;   
    activeStick.transform.position = strikingHand.position - grabPointOffset;   
}

Dominant Hand

The initial plan was to support right-handed players only. After discussions in the lectures around accessibility and player comfort, it became clear that giving the user control over their dominant hand is important. The final system supports both.

Pressing A spawns the stick with the right hand as the striking hand. Pressing X spawns it with the left. Pressing the same button again despawns the stick. Pressing the opposite button while the stick is active swaps the striking hand without despawning, letting the player switch on the fly.
