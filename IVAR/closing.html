Next Steps

Several concrete improvements emerged directly from user testing and presentation feedback.

  • Environment rework. The current environment uses vibrant colours and densely packed geometry close to the track, which contributed to cyber sickness reports. Replacing it with a calmer, more minimal setting would reduce visual noise and make rapid camera movement less disorienting.
  • Speed multiplier via UI. Ball speed is currently determined entirely by shot force, which requires consistent technique to achieve fast movement. A speed multiplier exposed through the in-game UI would let players opt into a faster or slower experience without needing to master the mechanics first.
  • Onboarding phase. Several system constraints like anchor mode requiring the ball to be stationary, directional filtering on the grab field, etc., were not obvious to first-time players. A short guided onboarding phase before the main map would surface these mechanics explicitly and reduce the friction that came from discovering them through failure.
  • Cue impact indicator and trajectory preview. Without feedback on where the cue will contact the ball or where the ball will travel, off-centre hits are percieved as sluggishness rather than a game mechanic. An impact point indicator on the ball surface and a projected trajectory line would give players the information they need to correct their aim and make the game more intuitive.

Reflection

This project was one of those where going beyond the bare minimum felt natural rather than obligatory. The course has come to an end, but after so many iterations and refinements there is still a clear sense of how much more could be added to improve the experience.

I registered for this course expecting a standard lecture series. It turned out to be something quite different, and I am glad it did. The small class size and discussion-based format made for a genuinely engaging environment, and the chance to see and critique each other's work throughout the semester added a lot. I would like to thank the course faculty and my classmates for making it such an enriching and supportive experience.
